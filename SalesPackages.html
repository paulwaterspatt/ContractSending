Tenancy Only Agreement


This agreement is made between the landlord of the Property (or Properties) and Keith Pattinson Ltd who agree to act as agent for the ‘Landlord’ and are hereinafter referred to as the ‘Agent’. The purpose of this document is to set out clearly and concisely the extent of the letting and management services offered and the scale of fees charged.


The terms of the Agreement set out in this document will constitute a binding legal contract. If you are unsure of your obligations under this Agreement, then you are advised to take independent legal advice before signing. This Agreement is set out to comply with the requirements of the Provision of Services Regulations 2009 (SI 2009/2999).


Tenancy Only

  1. Conducting a market appraisal and advising on likely rental income and letting potential.
  2. Advertising and general marketing of the property, including internal and external websites.
  3. Conducting viewings with prospective tenants.
  4. Undertaking tenant checks including employer / previous landlord references. Where necessary, a guarantor may be requested.
  5. Preparation of the tenancy agreement.
  6. Signing of the tenancy agreement and receiving the first month’s rent and deposit (or deposit replacement product).
  7. Payments will be made via direct bank transfer and a detailed rent statement will be forwarded to the Landlord (normally within 15 days of collection), less any fees or expenses due.

Fully Managed (Everything listed above plus)

  1. Lodging the deposit with the TDS (Tenancy Deposit Scheme) or setting up of deposit replacement product.
  2. Preparation of an inventory and schedule of condition.
  3. Undertaking a ‘check in’ at the start of the tenancy and ‘check out’ at the end of the tenancy.
  4. Renewing the tenancy agreement.
  5. Dealing with any deductions from the deposit at the end of the tenancy agreement (due to damage or rent arrears etc.)
  6. Notifying utility companies of the new tenant and any respective meter readings.
  7. Inspections of the Property are carried out on a periodic basis during the tenancy. Responsibility for, and management of, an empty property is not included, unless specially agreed in writing between the Landlord and the Agent.
  8. Co-ordination of repair and maintenance work including arranging for tradesmen to attend the property, obtaining estimates (where required), organizing works and settling accounts from rents received. Larger works may require pre-payment.
  9. Making payments on behalf of the Landlord from rents received for the costs in managing the property.
  10. Collection of rent. Any rent which is paid in advance will be held in our non-interest bearing client account and released to the landlord in accordance with the terms stated in the tenancy agreement. Typically this will be released monthly in advance.

General Authority

The landlord confirms that he/she is the sole or joint owner of the property and has the right and necessary permissions to rent out the property under the terms of the mortgage or head lease. Where necessary, the Landlord confirms that permission to let the property has been granted by the mortgagee and where appropriate, the freeholder, joints owner(s) or other relevant third party. The Landlord authorizes the Agent to carry out the various usual duties of property management detailed (but not limited to) the items listed on page 1. It is declared that the Agent may earn and retain commissions on insurance policies, other property related services, activities and maintenance works carried out, with no obligation for the landlord to use the Agent for these purposes.


This agreement is a rolling contract for the property detailed herein, (or any additional properties added to the Agreement via the “Additions” page found at the end of this Agreement). This means that should the property not be rented through The Agent, or if there is a period of non-rental, should the landlord wish to re---market and re---let the property through the Agent, this Agreement will stand, unless either party wishes to create a new Agreement detailing new terms and fees. For the avoidance of doubt, it should be noted that any properties on the “Additions” page, can only be added to this Agreement if they are to be let under the exact same service as the property within this Agreement. If they are not to be let under the same service, then a separate Agreement must be filled in and signed for that property.


This agreement will run until such time that either party gives notice to terminate the agreement in writing providing 30 days’ notice. If there is an active Tenancy in place, then the commission payable to the Agent until the end of that Tenancy Agreement will still be payable.


The Landlord and Tenant Act 1987 requires the Agent to include on all rent demands served on behalf the Landlord their full name and address in England or Wales at which notices and proceedings may be served on him. If the Landlord resides outside England or Wales and unless otherwise instructed, the Agent shall give their registered office or principal place of business as the address for service. The Agent will endeavor to forward any notices or proceedings to the Principal promptly; but cannot accept any responsibility for loss or damage caused either directly or indirectly as a result of this.


The Agent has the authority to sign the Tenancy Agreement and other relevant documentation (which pertain to our normal letting agent duties) on the landlord's behalf.


Our full fee becomes payable when a tenant we have introduced to the property either directly or indirectly commences a tenancy or takes up residency within a property you have instructed us to market for let (this would include if the landlord decides to deal with a potential tenant directly that we have introduced). Should a landlord withdraw from the lettings process after we have commenced referencing a potential tenant that the landlord indicated they would be happy to proceed with, we reserve the right to charge £300 + VAT (£360) to the landlord. This would also cover instances where the landlord changed their mind about a potential tenant (unless the tenant failed referencing).


Pattinson have teamed up with a company that offers a deposit replacement product / service which offers up to 8 weeks’ worth of cover as an alternative to a monetary deposit – normally in the form of an insurance guarantee. If a tenant chooses this product then there would be no monetary deposit to be paid or registered into a Tenancy Deposit Scheme and this would be reflected within the tenancy agreement.

TENANCY ONLY SERVICE


Where the Landlord does not wish the Agent to undertake full management (the Fully Managed Service), the Agent can provide a Tenancy Only Service. The Tenancy Only Service includes the points listed on page 1. The Landlord would remain responsible for all other aspects of the letting including collection of the rent, maintenance of the property and all other legal requirements. Where a deposit is taken, the Landlord would remain responsible for complying with the deposit protection requirements of the Housing Act 2004. Landlords are responsible for ensuring they lodge the deposit in line with current legislation and providing all relevant paperwork to the tenant. The agent is not responsible in the eventuality that the landlord fails to meet their legal requirements in this regard.


The fees are payable at the commencement of the tenancy and will be deducted from the monies received by the Agent on the Landlord’s behalf. If the tenant leaves prior to the end of the term of the tenancy, through no fault of the Agent, the Landlord shall not be entitled to reimbursement of any fees paid.
